🤖 NMHS AI Readiness Assessment

AI Readiness — Diagnostic Tool

A structured, open-source assessment of your NMHS's readiness to adopt and deploy AI across six critical pillars. Free to use, adapt, and share.

How to use this tool: Complete this assessment as a structured workshop or interview with NMHS technical and management staff (allow 2–3 hours). Score each item 0–3, add observations or evidence in the notes field, and the tool will calculate your weighted score and maturity tier automatically. Use the results to identify priority development areas and guide your AI roadmap.
Scoring Scale
Each of the 60 items is rated on a four-point scale.
0
Absent
Not in place. No awareness or activity in this area.
1
Initial
Awareness exists but activity is ad hoc or incomplete.
2
Developing
Elements in place but not fully operational or systematised.
3
Established
Fully operational and consistently applied.

Weighted scoring: Weighted Score = (Pillar Score ÷ Pillar Maximum) × Pillar Weight. Scores are summed for an overall result out of 100. Pillar 1 has a maximum of 36 (12 items × 3); all others are 30 (10 items × 3). This drives the maturity tier: Tier 1 Foundational (0–33), Tier 2 Developing (34–66), Tier 3 Advanced (67–100).

🚀 AI Pilots

Suggested AI Pilots — Prioritised

Starting points recommended by operational impact, technical feasibility, and alignment with available partner tools. Check prerequisites against your pillar scores.

Prerequisites: Each pilot lists checklist item scores that should be in place before starting. Cross-reference with your pillar scores — items that scored 0 or 1 in the relevant prerequisites are your most important preparation steps.
1
ML-based bias correction of NWP output
Temperature & precipitation post-processing using ECMWF AIFS
HIGH
Lowest data and compute requirements of all pilots; highest operational value per unit of effort; well-documented methods; immediately deployable with Phase 1 actions. Expected 10–20% skill improvement in 24–72h forecasts.
Prerequisites: 1.1 ≥2 (historical data), 2.3 ≥1 (Python workstations), 3.1 ≥2 (Python-proficient staff)  ·  Partners: ECMWF, regional partner NMHS
2
Radar-based precipitation nowcasting
Deep learning or optical flow methods — MeteoSwiss RainForest applicable
HIGH
Nowcasting is the highest-impact service for flash flood warning. MeteoSwiss RainForest is directly applicable. Even without dense radar, gauge-based QPE components are usable.
Prerequisites: 1.3 ≥2 (near real-time AWS), 2.7 ≥2 (radar data), 3.2 ≥1 (ML training)  ·  Partners: MeteoSwiss, EUMETNET OPERA
3
AI-enhanced hydrological flood forecasting
ML river level prediction for key national catchments
HIGH
Directly addresses the highest-risk hydrological hazard. Shared catchments with neighbouring countries create bilateral co-operation opportunities. Oxford hydrology group actively seeks this type of collaboration.
Prerequisites: 1.2 ≥2 (hydrological data), 4.6 ≥1 (hydro forecasting), 3.2 ≥1 (ML training)  ·  Partners: University of Oxford, World Bank, SMHI, regional partner NMHS
4
Agrometeorological advisory using AI
Drought index prediction, soil moisture forecasting
MEDIUM
High demand from agriculture sector. Partially available data. Aligns with national digital transformation priorities and relevant policy frameworks. Oxford ECI actively engages on applied climate services of this type.
Prerequisites: 1.1 ≥2 (historical data), 4.3 ≥1 (probabilistic methods), 5.4 ≥1 (policy alignment)  ·  Partners: FAO, UNDP, Ministry of Agriculture, Oxford ECI
5
Automated weather product generation
NLP-based text forecast drafting from NWP data
LOWER
Reduces forecaster workload but requires a mature NWP pipeline and QA frameworks. Best pursued after Pilots 1–3 are established. Requires strong operational workflow foundations.
Prerequisites: 4.1 ≥2 (structured workflow), 4.7 ≥2 (verification system), 5.3 ≥1 (AI guidelines)  ·  Partners: WMO, private sector NLP providers
Two critical first actions regardless of tier:
1. Register for ECMWF Open Data and begin accessing AIFS forecast output for your domain — this costs nothing and can start this week at ecmwf.int
2. Contact the EUMETNET Secretariat to request E-AI observer participation — info@eumetnet.eu
📚 Resources

Key Resources & Links

Official access points, training programmes and contacts for ECMWF, MeteoSwiss, University of Oxford, EUMETNET, EUMETSAT and WMO.

ECMWF
ECMWF
AIFS Open Data Portal
Free, real-time AIFS forecast data under CC-4.0-BY. Both AIFS Single and AIFS ENS available. Start here this week.
ecmwf.int/aifs-data
ECMWF
eLearning Portal
Free self-paced courses including DestinE ML for Earth Systems (3-part series) and the 6-part Discover Anemoi webinar series.
learning.ecmwf.int
ECMWF
ML Weather Prediction Course
Annual 5-day course (typically October). Open to non-member states. Apply via events.ecmwf.int — deadline ~July each year.
events.ecmwf.int
ECMWF
Anemoi Framework
Open-source ML toolkit (Apache 2.0) for dataset creation, model training, and inference deployment. Co-developed with MeteoSwiss, KNMI and others.
github.com/ecmwf/anemoi-core
MeteoSwiss
MeteoSwiss
RainForest — Open-Source QPE
Operational random forest quantitative precipitation estimation. Open-source Python on GitHub. Adaptable to local data even without dense radar.
github.com/MeteoSwiss/rainforest
MeteoSwiss
Open Data & International Cooperation
OGD documentation and bilateral collaboration enquiries. MeteoSwiss co-leads the EUMETNET E-AI programme and actively seeks tool-transfer partnerships.
opendatadocs.meteoswiss.ch
University of Oxford
University of Oxford
Predictability of Weather & Climate Group
AI weather models applied in East Africa with ECMWF and WFP. Actively seeks applied collaborations with data-constrained NMHSs. Direct email — no formal portal.
physics.ox.ac.uk
University of Oxford
Environmental Change Institute
Applied climate research partnerships and climate services design. Seasonal outlook and agrometeorological collaborations. Send a one-page concept note directly.
eci.ox.ac.uk
EUMETNET, EUMETSAT & WMO
EUMETNET
E-AI Programme
The unifying framework for AI in European NMHSs. Write to info@eumetnet.eu requesting E-AI observer or participant status, referencing WMO membership.
eumetnet.eu/AI
EUMETSAT
E-AI Bursaries
Annual bursary call (deadline ~February). Non-member NMHSs must partner with a EUMETSAT member-state lead institution as co-applicant.
eumetsat.int
WMO
WIPPS Pilot Projects & JAG-AI
WMO pilot project fund for AI in NMHSs targeting low- and middle-income countries. Joint Advisory Group on AI (JAG-AI) guidance documents for NMHSs.
wmo.int
ECMWF / Hugging Face
AIFS Model Weights
AIFS Single v1.0 model weights on Hugging Face. Run inference locally on your own hardware. Also PeakWeather dataset from MeteoSwiss for training nowcasting models.
huggingface.co/ecmwf
All details are current as of March 2026. Verify directly with each institution before acting — programme deadlines, contacts and access conditions change. The AI landscape in operational meteorology is evolving rapidly.
